Core features

Conversational intake
Tell AI how you want to be understood — or let it ask. Questions scale in depth with your mode. You're always in control of what you share.

Upload a photo. AI gives honest, specific feedback on body shape, skin condition, hair, outfit, and overall style. Filtered in Relaxed. Raw in Beast.

Your full picture. Style, social presence, personality, vibe. What's working. What isn't. Exactly what to change. Specific, not generic — think YouTube glow-up energy.

Designer remembers you. As you level up, the AI updates your profile and adjusts advice. Come back, update it, watch your score move.
05
Vibe profile
AI builds a profile of how you come across — not just how you look. Personality, energy, social presence. The full picture of what makes you, you — and what doesn't.

AI system design
Intake prompt — on first use
Structured prompt to Claude with: mode level, user's self-description, photo analysis output, and stated goals. Claude returns a full profile JSON — category scores, top strengths, top weaknesses, prioritised action list. Stored and referenced for every future check-in.
Check-in prompt — on return
Pull stored profile + previous scores + any new photos or updates from the user. Ask Claude: "Given this person's profile and what has changed, what's their updated score and what should they focus on next?" Returns delta score, updated advice, and encouragement calibrated to mode.
Mode tone system — five system prompts
One system prompt per mode. Relaxed = warm older sibling, lots of encouragement, leads with positives. Real = honest friend, balanced. Locked in = direct coach. Beast = little filter, high standards, pushes hard. Brutal = actively critical, looks for what's wrong, encourages but never softens the truth. Each is tested independently before launch.
Tech stack
Frontend Next.js Web-first. WKWebView shell for iOS. Share as much code as possible across platforms.
AI Claude Sonnet 4.6 Vision for photo analysis, text for conversational intake and audit generation. Stream the audit output — watching it generate is part of the experience.
Database Supabase profiles, scores, check_ins, photo_analyses tables. Score history for tracking over time.
Auth Prism ID Same login as all Prism apps. No separate account creation.
Storage Supabase Storage User photos in private buckets. Never used for model training. Deleted on account removal.
Rate limiting Upstash Redis Free: 3 AI sessions/day. Pro: unlimited. Vision calls are expensive — cost model before launch.
Suite connections
Prism Mood
Low mood score shifts AI tone to warmer and more encouraging regardless of chosen mode. Don't critique someone who's already down.
Prism Grind
Glow-up roadmap items can be sent to Grind as active goals. Style goals become daily missions.
Prism Roast
Roast can audit your style or social presence and send results to Designer as a check-in prompt. Honest feedback meets the full picture.
Prism Social
Share your score milestone or glow-up progress as a post. Shareable score cards — "I just hit 74 in Beast mode." Built-in virality.

Note: Apps are currently standalone. Suite integration is planned but not required for MVP.

Roadmap
1

MVP — text intake + audit

Conversational intake, Claude audit generation, score display, Relaxed and Real modes. Web only. Get the core loop right — honest AI feedback that actually helps.

2

Photo analysis + Beast mode

Vision input live. Photo analysis across all visual dimensions. Beast mode unlocked. Score tracking over time. Glow-up roadmap generation.

3

Ongoing check-ins + monetisation

AI remembers you across sessions. Score history and progression curve. Designer Pro paywall. Rate limiting and cost controls.

4

iOS + Brutal mode + suite

iOS app. Brutal mode goes live (experimental gate). Social sharing of score milestones. Prism Pass integration. Grind and Mood connections.

Open questions
?

Photo privacy and safety User photos for AI analysis are sensitive. Explicit policy needed: not stored long-term, not used for training, deleted on request. Must be clear in onboarding before any photo is uploaded.

?

Brutal mode safety review A mode with negative filter that critiques appearance needs careful testing before launch. Define where the AI draws the line — honest critique vs harmful content. Review required before shipping.

?

Score gaming Users could game intake questions to inflate their score. Consider locking score adjustments to photo check-ins or time-gating score changes to once per week.

?

API cost model Vision calls + long audit prompts per user session could get expensive fast. Model the cost per session at each mode level before setting free tier limits.

?

Body image considerations App analyses appearance. Needs thoughtful UX guardrails — no language that encourages harmful comparisons, and a clear stance that the goal is confidence not perfection.
